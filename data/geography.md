# Geography Strategy

Three geographic buckets. Prioritize A and B equally. C is deprioritized.

**Runtime override:** If user specifies geography (e.g., "только Квебек", "only Montreal"), restrict ALL searches to that geography for this run.

## Bucket A: Canada-based roles (high priority)

Any Canadian city — candidate open to relocation anywhere in Canada (hybrid/on-site) and remote-Canada.
Key cities: Montreal, Toronto, Vancouver, Calgary, Ottawa, Quebec City.

**Salary rules:**
- Montreal-based or remote (no relocation): no minimum. Always record salary but don't filter by it.
- Relocation required (outside Montreal, hybrid/on-site): minimum CAD 130K. Below → still record, add note "Below relocation minimum".

**Identification signals:** Canadian city in listing, salary in CAD, RRSP benefits (not 401k), CNESST compliance.

## Bucket B: US companies hiring remotely from Canada (high priority)

Highest compensation ROI. Montreal EST aligns with US East Coast. Remote — no relocation, no minimum salary.

**Hiring arrangements:** Canadian subsidiary, EOR (Deel/Remote.com), independent contractor (avoid if possible).

**Green flags:** explicit "Canada" in location, RRSP/Canadian benefits, salary in CAD, Canadian employees on LinkedIn, known Canadian entity.

**Red flags:** 401(k) only, "United States" in fine print, US work authorization required, compensation benchmarked to US cities only.

## Bucket C: US roles requiring relocation/visa (low priority)

Deprioritized — TN visa tightening (June 2025). "Software Engineer" not on TN profession list.
Better strategy: get hired at company with Canadian + US offices, work from Canada, explore L-1 transfer.
US-only to monitor (not actively apply): Retool, LaunchDarkly, Vercel, Hex, Palantir, Bloomberg.
